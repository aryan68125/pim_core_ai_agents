# PIM-AI — 8 Core Features

**Version:** 1.0 | **Audience:** Business Stakeholders & Executives | **Date:** April 2026

Each feature addresses a specific business challenge, resolves it with AI, and delivers measurable benefits.

---

## 01 — Product Description Generator
**Category:** AI Writing

### Business Challenge
- Writing descriptions for thousands of SKUs requires large copywriting teams
- Inconsistent tone and quality across categories and channels
- New product launches delayed waiting for content approval
- SEO is deprioritised due to time pressure, hurting organic traffic
- High cost of external agencies for seasonal catalog refreshes

### How PIM-AI Resolves It
- Auto-generates titles and descriptions from attributes and images in seconds
- Brand voice and tone configured once, applied consistently at scale
- SEO keywords embedded automatically based on category signals
- Drafts ready for review — human effort shifts from writing to approving
- Multi-language output removes translation bottleneck

### Business Benefits
- 80%+ reduction in content production time
- Lower copywriting and agency costs
- Consistent brand voice across all channels
- Improved organic search rankings and traffic
- Faster product launches with no content backlog

## 02 — Auto Product Tagging
**Category:** Vision AI

### Business Challenge
- Merchandisers manually assign tags to every product — a slow, error-prone task
- Inconsistent tagging leads to poor search and filter results for customers
- New supplier products arrive with minimal or no attribute data
- Missing tags cause products to be invisible in filtered browsing
- Scaling the catalog compounds the problem rapidly

### How PIM-AI Resolves It
- Vision AI analyses product images to extract colour, material, style, shape
- Combines image and text signals for high-confidence tagging
- Maps tags to existing attribute schema automatically
- Low-confidence tags routed for human review only
- Runs across entire catalog in batch — no manual queue

### Business Benefits
- Up to 80% reduction in manual tagging effort
- Customers find products faster — lower bounce rate
- Higher add-to-cart from improved filter accuracy
- Merchandiser time freed for strategic work
- Consistent attribute coverage across all SKUs

## 03 — Auto Classification
**Category:** Classification AI

### Business Challenge
- Products placed in wrong categories cause marketplace rejections
- Manual classification is subjective and inconsistent across teams
- Complex category hierarchies (GS1, eCl@ss) require specialist knowledge
- Mis-categorised products hurt customer browsing and trust
- Re-classification cycles are costly and time-consuming

### How PIM-AI Resolves It
- AI maps products to the right category using descriptions and attributes
- Supports GS1, eCl@ss, and custom internal taxonomy structures
- Confidence scoring surfaces borderline cases for review
- Learns from corrections to improve over time
- Processes new supplier products automatically on ingestion

### Business Benefits
- Fewer marketplace listing rejections
- Improved catalog integrity and reporting accuracy
- Reduced compliance risk in regulated industries
- Faster supplier onboarding with auto-classification at intake
- Consistent categorisation across all teams and regions

## 04 — Image Sequencing
**Category:** Automation

### Business Challenge
- Images uploaded by suppliers in random or incorrect order
- Each marketplace has different image sequence requirements
- Manual reordering across thousands of products is unsustainable
- Lifestyle or detail images appearing as hero shots reduce conversion
- No consistent enforcement of brand image standards at scale

### How PIM-AI Resolves It
- AI identifies image type: hero, lifestyle, detail, infographic, spec sheet
- Category-level rules define the required sequence for each product type
- Images auto-reordered on upload — no manual intervention needed
- Marketplace-specific rulesets (Amazon, Zalando, etc.) supported
- Flags images that don't meet sequence or quality thresholds

### Business Benefits
- Higher conversion from correct hero image presentation
- Consistent brand standards enforced automatically
- Zero manual reordering effort across the catalog
- Marketplace compliance without manual checking
- Faster product publishing cycle end-to-end

- Background removal and white/neutral background replacement
- Clutter, watermarks, and distracting elements removed
- Colour correction and white balance normalisation
- Batch processes entire supplier image sets overnight

### Business Benefits
- Significantly lower photography and editing costs
- Consistent, professional image quality across all listings
- Higher conversion from better product presentation
- Faster time-to-publish without waiting for reshoots
- Creative team freed from repetitive editing tasks

## 06 — Image-to-Product Data
**Category:** OCR + AI

### Business Challenge
- CPG and FMCG teams manually re-key data from supplier labels
- Ingredients, allergens, and nutritional info must be 100% accurate
- Suppliers deliver product info as images or PDFs — not structured data
- Human transcription errors create compliance and safety risks
- Label updates from suppliers require manual catalog corrections

### How PIM-AI Resolves It
- OCR + AI reads product labels and packaging images automatically
- Extracts nutritional values, ingredients, claims, certifications, allergens
- Structures extracted data into your PIM attribute schema
- Validates data against regulatory compliance rules
- Flags discrepancies between label data and existing records

### Business Benefits
- Eliminates manual re-keying of label data entirely
- Reduces compliance and safety risk from transcription errors
- Accelerates supplier onboarding in CPG / FMCG significantly
- Instantly structured, usable product data from any packaging image
- Supports regulatory audit trails with source image traceability

## 08 — Agentic Commerce
**Category:** Agentic AI

### What It Is
An internal AI-powered layer that enables autonomous agents to manage, query, and act on catalog, pricing, and inventory decisions on behalf of business procurement teams — without human intervention at every step.

### Business Challenge
- Procurement teams manually query catalogs, compare specs, and validate pricing
- Catalog, pricing, and inventory data sits in silos — agents can't act without clean APIs
- Procurement decisions are delayed by human approval at every routine step
- No intelligent layer to match procurement intent with the right product automatically
- Reordering, substitution, and availability checks require manual effort

### How PIM-AI Resolves It
- Exposes a structured catalog API that AI agents can query in real time
- Conversational discovery interface lets agents resolve complex procurement queries in natural language
- AI agents autonomously compare products, validate against procurement rules, and select the best match
- Full transaction and checkout layer allows agents to raise purchase orders without manual handoff
- Inventory and pricing signals feed agents so substitutions and reorders happen automatically

### Three Core Capabilities PIM-AI Provides to Agents

| Capability | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Structured Catalog API | Clean, queryable product data — specs, variants, pricing, and availability |
| Conversational Discovery | Natural language interface for complex procurement queries |
| Transaction & Checkout Layer | Agents raise purchase orders and complete transactions end-to-end |

### Business Benefits
- Procurement cycles reduced from days to minutes
- Fewer human touchpoints on routine reorders
- Real-time pricing and inventory decisions eliminate stock-outs
- Consistent application of procurement rules across every transaction
- Positions the business at the forefront of AI-driven commerce infrastructure

## New Product Onboarding Workflow

When a new product is ingested, the agent runs this sequence:

```
classify → tag → generate description → sequence images → check quality
```

Output: a publish-ready product record, or flagged for human review.
